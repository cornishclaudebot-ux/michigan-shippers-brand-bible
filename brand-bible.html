03 / The Logo
The mark

The logo

An isometric box, lined like a fresh print pulling off the press and grounded by a press-blue base. It reads as a package and a printed label at once, the two things this company has always made. Keep it confident, keep it square, keep it clear.

Stacked · on paper
Knockout · on navy
The mark · alone

Clear space & minimum size

Hold clear space of at least the height of the box mark on every side. Never crowd the logo with text, photos, or other logos. Keep the wordmark legible: never place the horizontal lockup below about 130px wide on screen, or 1 inch in print.

Always

  • Lead with the full lockup, mark plus wordmark, for first impressions.
  • Use the knockout version on navy and other dark grounds.
  • Keep the box mark's print lines and blue base intact.
  • Give it room to breathe.

Never

  • Recolor the mark or swap the press blue for another color.
  • Stretch, skew, rotate, or add drop shadows and outlines.
  • Rebuild the wordmark in a different typeface.
  • Place it on a busy photo with no clear, calm backing.

05 / Type
The type system

Typography

Two typefaces do all the work, and they're built into every device, so the brand reads the same everywhere with nothing to download. Serif for heritage and headlines, sans for clean, plain talk.

Display · headlines · the wordmark
Georgia
ABCDEFGHIJKLMNOPQRSTUVWXYZ · 0123456789
Use for: headlines, the MICHIGAN SHIPPERS wordmark, big statements, and pull quotes. It carries the 67 years. Set it in navy, bold, with comfortable line height.
Body · labels · everything readable
Helvetica / System Sans
ABCDEFGHIJKLMNOPQRSTUVWXYZ abcdefghijklmnop · 0123456789
Use for: body copy, captions, forms, and the SUPPLY · EST. 1959 line under the wordmark, set in wide letter-spaced caps. This is the voice talking plainly. Weights 400 to 800.
One rule above all: don't mix in random fonts. Georgia and the system sans cover every job. The wordmark's caps-and-spacing treatment of "SUPPLY · EST. 1959" is part of the logo, never retype it freehand in body copy.

10 / Standards
The rules

Do & don't

The shortlist that keeps every quote, label, web page, and conversation unmistakably Michigan Shippers Supply.

✓ Always

  • Lead with heritage: family-owned since 1959.
  • Press Blue on Heritage Cream, anchored by Brand Navy.
  • Georgia for headlines, system sans for everything readable.
  • Talk plainly and reassure on compliance.
  • Show real printed product as proof.
  • Give the customer the right press, never an upsell.
  • Reserve gold for the White-Glove program.

✗ Never

  • Sound corporate, hypey, or high-pressure.
  • Recolor, stretch, or rebuild the logo.
  • Compete on being "the cheapest click."
  • Use gold or "white-glove" language off-program.
  • Mix in random fonts or stock mockups.
  • Run inconsistent name, address, or phone anywhere.
  • Make a maker feel small for ordering small.
A note on the name: the brand and marketing name is Michigan Shippers Supply (no apostrophe), matching the logo and the website. The legal name, Michigan Shippers' Supply, Inc., keeps its apostrophe for contracts and invoices. Pick one in public and stay consistent, mismatched name, address, and phone data is the single most common thing that buries a local business in search.
